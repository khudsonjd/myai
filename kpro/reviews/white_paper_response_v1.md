# Response to Keith — White Paper Review

[Keith],

*Note: This response was drafted in collaboration with my AI assistant as part of an iterative review process.*

Thank you for sharing this — it's a compelling read and the core argument is one I believe in. A few thoughts from my experience working with both platforms:

**On the feedback loop**

The paper frames the advantage as a technology gap, but I think the stronger argument is a *complexity-proportionality* one. As solution complexity increases, PowerApps demands disproportionately more — build time, maintenance overhead, and technical depth. The knowledge and effort curve steepens with every layer. With mDFFS, that curve stays nearly flat. The citizen developer isn't just faster at the start — they stay faster *and* remain the person who can actually maintain what they built. That's the sustainable advantage, and I think it's worth stating explicitly.

**On mDFFS vs Power Apps**

The complementary framing is right, and I'd keep it. I'd add one nuance: the ceiling on mDFFS is higher than the paper suggests. A more experienced developer working in mDFFS can take on significantly more complex scenarios than the "SharePoint-native forms" framing implies. That's not a weakness of the positioning — it's an upside worth mentioning.

**On the governance model**

The proposed model is well-designed and I think it's a worthy framework to test. I'd suggest characterizing it as a *proposed* model rather than a proven one — the distinction matters to technical readers and protects credibility with skeptics. If there are organizations piloting it, even early results would strengthen this section considerably.

**One broader note**

The document reads in places more like a sales brief than a traditional white paper — which may be entirely intentional for your audience. Just flagging it in case you're targeting readers who will respond better to a more neutral analytical frame. Happy to discuss if that's a direction worth considering.

Overall — strong piece. The central argument is sound and the examples land well.

Kerry
