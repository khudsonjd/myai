## Technology & Methodology Stances

### mDFFS / Citizen Development

**The feedback loop claim (technology gap)**
The gap isn't *just* technology — it's also maintainability and the proportional cost of complexity. A less technical person can build a faster solution *and* sustain it over time. The key insight: as solution complexity increases, PowerApps requires disproportionately more build time, maintenance effort, and technical knowledge — the curve steepens. mDFFS complexity cost stays nearly flat by comparison. This proportionality argument is stronger than what the white paper states.

**mDFFS vs Power Apps positioning**
Complementary framing is correct, but undersells mDFFS. A more experienced developer can take mDFFS significantly further than the paper implies — it's not just a "SharePoint-native forms" tool at that skill level. The ceiling is higher than presented.

**Governance model**
A worthy and interesting design, but not yet implemented or proven in practice. Should be characterized as a proposed model, not a validated one.

**Sales document tone**
The paper reads as a sales/marketing document as much as a white paper — written by a consulting firm selling the methodology. Worth flagging professionally in any response.
